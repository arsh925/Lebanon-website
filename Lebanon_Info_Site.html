Culture

Lebanon's culture is a blend of ancient and modern influences, shaped by Phoenician, Roman, Ottoman, and French histories. It is known for its art, music, literature, and lively festivals.

Religion

Lebanon is religiously diverse, with significant populations of Christians, Sunni and Shia Muslims, and Druze. The country respects freedom of religion and has a unique power-sharing political system based on religious affiliation.

Language

Arabic is the official language, but French and English are widely spoken. Many Lebanese people are trilingual, switching easily between languages in conversation.

Food

Lebanese cuisine is famous worldwide. Popular dishes include hummus, tabbouleh, kibbeh, fattoush, and manakish. Meals are often shared, reflecting the social nature of Lebanese dining.

Traditions

Lebanon has rich traditions involving music, dance (like the dabke), weddings, and family celebrations. Hospitality is a core value, and guests are always treated with generosity.

Infrastructure

Lebanon's infrastructure includes a network of roads, ports, and telecommunications. While some areas face challenges due to economic issues, cities like Beirut still showcase modern architecture and development.
